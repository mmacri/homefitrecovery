How to Choose the Right Compression Gear

Understand Compression Levels

Compression is measured in mmHg (millimeters of mercury):

  • 15-20 mmHg: Mild compression, good for everyday wear and light recovery
  • 20-30 mmHg: Moderate compression, ideal for most recovery needs
  • 30+ mmHg: Firm compression, best for serious athletes or medical needs

Consider Your Specific Needs

Different activities and recovery goals require different compression gear:

  • Running/Cardio: Focus on calf sleeves or compression socks
  • Weightlifting: Consider compression shirts or upper body gear
  • Overall Recovery: Full-length tights or recovery-specific gear
  • Joint Issues: Targeted compression with added support features

Frequently Asked Questions

Compression gear offers multiple recovery benefits including improved blood circulation, reduced muscle soreness and swelling, and faster removal of metabolic waste products. The graduated pressure they provide helps blood flow back to the heart, which can accelerate the healing process after intense workouts.

For recovery purposes, wear compression gear immediately after workout sessions for 1-3 hours, or overnight for maximum benefits. Many athletes also use compression during workouts to support muscles and improve performance, though recovery-specific benefits are most pronounced post-exercise.

Compression gear should be snug but not uncomfortably tight. You should feel gentle, consistent pressure throughout the targeted area, but it shouldn't restrict movement or cause pain. If the gear leaves deep marks on your skin or causes numbness/tingling, it's too tight. Follow manufacturer sizing guides carefully.
