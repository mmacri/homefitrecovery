Massage Gun Buyer's Guide

How to Choose the Right Massage Gun

Selecting the ideal massage gun depends on your specific needs, budget, and how you plan to use it. Here are the key factors to consider when making your decision:

  • Power & Amplitude:

    For deep tissue work and serious athletes, look for 12-16mm amplitude and high stall force (30+ lbs). For general use or sensitive areas, lower power options may be sufficient.

  • Battery Life:

    Consider how often and how long you'll use the device. Most quality guns offer 2-3 hours of battery life per charge, but heavy users might want removable batteries.

  • Noise Level:

    Lower amplitude guns tend to be quieter. If you'll use your massage gun in shared spaces or quiet environments, noise level becomes an important factor.

  • Attachment Options:

    Different attachments serve different purposes: round heads for large muscle groups, bullet tips for trigger points, flat heads for general use, and fork attachments for the spine and neck.

When to Use a Massage Gun

Pre-Workout

Use a massage gun for 30-60 seconds per muscle group before exercise to improve blood flow, increase range of motion, and prepare muscles for activity. Use a lower speed setting.

Post-Workout

After exercise, use for 1-2 minutes per muscle group to reduce soreness, aid recovery, and decrease lactic acid buildup. Moderate speeds work best.

For Pain Relief

For chronic pain or stiff muscles, use the massage gun for up to 2 minutes on lower speeds, focusing on the affected area and surrounding muscles.

For Relaxation

To relieve general tension or stress, use the massage gun on lower speeds for a gentle full-body session lasting 10-15 minutes.
